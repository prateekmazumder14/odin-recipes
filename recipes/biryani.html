Biryani Recipe

Go Back

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500 grams chicken (or mutton, beef, or vegetables)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Whole spices (1 bay leaf, 4-5 cloves, 2-3 cardamom pods, 1 cinnamon stick)
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water (for cooking rice)

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain the water before cooking.
  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil, add salt and the soaked rice. Cook the rice until it's about 70-80% done, then drain and set aside.
  3. In another large pot or pan, heat oil and ghee. Add the whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon). Fry them until fragrant.
  4. Add sliced onions to the pot and fry them until they turn golden brown. Set half of the fried onions aside for garnishing.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil begins to separate.
  7. Add the chicken (or choice of meat/vegetables) and cook until it is browned and cooked through. Add yogurt and mix well, simmer for a few minutes.
  8. Now, layer the partially cooked rice over the meat mixture. Sprinkle chopped mint and coriander leaves on top, followed by the fried onions you set aside earlier.
  9. Cover the pot with a tight lid. Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and rice to finish cooking (you can also place a heavy pan under the pot to prevent burning).
  10. Once done, remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving. Serve hot with raita or a salad of your choice!

Enjoy! 😁
